# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Enterprise decision-makers and security executives at Fortune 500s, banks, and telcos. They operate under regulatory pressure, carry responsibility for infrastructure failure, and have seen vendors overpromise. When they land on this site they are already in problem-solving mode — they are not browsing, they are evaluating. They distrust obvious marketing. They respect demonstration of competence over assertion of it.

## Product Purpose

Heil Tech is an elite cybersecurity firm offering vulnerability assessment, compliance auditing (SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001), and advanced cyber defense. The site exists to convert qualified enterprise prospects into contacted leads. Success looks like a senior security officer submitting the contact form with the phrase "we need to talk."

## Brand Personality

Technical. Relentless. Invisible.

The brand operates like a red team unit: no wasted motion, no loose talk, always ahead of the adversary. They are the firm that Fortune 500 boards call when the situation is serious, not the vendor that cold-emails procurement. The voice is confident to the point of understatement — they do not need to convince you they are good; their record does.

## Anti-references

- **Cheap hacker aesthetic**: Matrix rain, neon green on black, hooded figure stock photos, glitch effects used decoratively. This signals amateur, not elite.
- **Generic SaaS vendor**: Soft purple/teal gradients, "Scale with confidence" microcopy, feature grids with emoji. Loses all technical credibility.
- **Corporate IT vendor**: Navy + gray palette, clip-art shields, stock photos of server rooms and handshakes. Signals mid-tier managed services, not special operations.

## Design Principles

1. **Operate below the noise.** Every element earns its pixel. Credibility accumulates through restraint, not decoration. When in doubt, remove.

2. **Show craft, not claims.** Enterprise clients distrust boasts. Technical precision in the interface itself signals technical precision in the work. The site's construction is part of the pitch.

3. **The elite don't recruit.** The tone is confident and selective, not urgent or sales-y. The site assumes the visitor already knows they have a problem; the question is whether Heil Tech will accept the engagement.

4. **Military precision over hacker theater.** The aesthetic is aerospace/defense HUD, not pop-culture hacking. Every visual decision should feel like it belongs in a SCIF, not a CTF writeup.

5. **Invisible infrastructure.** What Heil Tech does is most visible when nothing goes wrong. The design carries that same quality: seamless, inevitable, present only when needed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal requirements specified. Best-effort: sufficient contrast for key text, keyboard-navigable CTAs, no motion for users who prefer reduced motion.
